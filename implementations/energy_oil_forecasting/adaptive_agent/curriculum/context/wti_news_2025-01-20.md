### Price Level and Recent Trend
WTI crude oil prices experienced a notable upward trend in early January 2025, recovering from a December 2024 average of $69.70 per barrel. By January 10, 2025, NYMEX WTI crude was trading around $76.57 per barrel, and it climbed further to settle at $80.04 per barrel on January 15. According to the Energy Information Administration (EIA), Cushing WTI spot prices averaged $74.31 per barrel during the first full week of January and rose to an average of $78.20 per barrel for the week ending January 17. This price strength was primarily supported by colder winter temperatures boosting heating fuel demand and persistent geopolitical tensions.

### OPEC+ Production Decisions
To counter market uncertainty and defend prices, the OPEC+ alliance made a key policy adjustment during its ministerial meeting on December 5, 2024. The group decided to postpone the gradual rollback of its 2.2 million barrels per day (mb/d) voluntary production cuts by three months, delaying the start of the unwinding from January 2025 to April 2025. Additionally, the alliance extended its 1.65 mb/d voluntary cuts (originally agreed in April 2023) through the end of December 2026, while extending the compensation timeline for overproducing members to June 2026. Despite these commitments, reports emerged in early January that Russia had missed its OPEC+ output targets, adding some friction to the group's efforts.

### Geopolitical Supply Risks
Geopolitical developments remained a central driver of supply concerns and market volatility. On January 10, 2025, the United States announced fresh sanctions targeting Russia's oil sector to further restrict its export revenues. Despite these measures, Russian oil exports remained resilient, dipping only slightly to 7.33 mb/d in December 2024. Meanwhile, supply fears in the Middle East eased slightly in mid-January amid ongoing talks of a potential ceasefire between Israel and Hamas, which tempered concerns of a broader regional conflict disrupting flows.

### Demand Outlook and Analyst Forecasts
The demand outlook for 2025 showed modest optimism but remained constrained by macroeconomic headwinds. In its January 15, 2025 report, the International Energy Agency (IEA) noted that global oil demand rose seasonally by a robust 1.5 mb/d in Q4 2024, prompting it to forecast demand growth of 1.05 mb/d for 2025. OPEC and the EIA held more optimistic demand growth forecasts for 2025 at 1.4 mb/d and 1.3 mb/d, respectively. However, analysts warned of a looming supply surplus, as non-OPEC+ production—led by the U.S., Brazil, and Guyana—was projected to expand by 1.5 to 1.7 mb/d in 2025. Technical analysts also cautioned that WTI was facing strong resistance around $77.70 per barrel, warning that a failure to break through could trigger a pullback toward support levels near $67.20 per barrel.
