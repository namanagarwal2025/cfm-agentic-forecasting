**Price Level and Recent Trend**
WTI crude oil prices closed out 2024 and entered early January 2025 trading in the high $60s to low $70s per barrel. After a volatile year that saw prices peak near $87 in April and drop as low as $65.75 in September, WTI settled around $71.00 per barrel by the end of December. However, a late-December rally pushed WTI above its key technical resistance of $71.33, entering the first week of January 2025 with upward momentum as bulls attempted to break a six-month downtrend.

**OPEC+ Production Decisions**
To combat downward price pressures, OPEC+ made a significant policy decision during its 38th Ministerial Meeting on December 5, 2024. The alliance agreed to extend its group-wide 2 million barrels per day (bpd) production cuts through the end of 2026. Crucially, the eight member nations implementing voluntary cuts of 2.2 million bpd—including Saudi Arabia and Russia—agreed to delay the unwinding of these cuts until April 2025, planning a gradual phase-out through September 2026. This marked the third postponement of the output hike, reflecting the cartel's proactive stance on market stability.

**Geopolitical Supply Risks and Demand Outlook**
Geopolitical supply risks remain a key focal point, with ongoing tensions in the Middle East and potential shifts in international sanctions against Russia and Venezuela maintaining a modest risk premium. On the demand side, the International Energy Agency (IEA) raised its 2025 global oil demand growth forecast in December 2024 to 1.1 million bpd, bringing total projected demand to 103.9 million bpd. This upward revision was driven by anticipated economic stimulus in China and steady growth in the US. However, the IEA emphasized that the market would remain "comfortably supplied" due to surging non-OPEC+ production from the US, Brazil, Guyana, and Canada.

**Notable Analyst Forecasts**
Prominent market analysts and agencies are forecasting a softer pricing environment for 2025. In its December 2024 Short-Term Energy Outlook (STEO), the US Energy Information Administration (EIA) lowered its WTI spot price forecast to average $69.12 per barrel in 2025. Similarly, J.P. Morgan Commodities Research projected WTI to average $69 per barrel in 2025 and drop further to $57 in 2026. Fitch Ratings also expected a downward trend, forecasting Brent to decrease to $70 per barrel in 2025 due to moderating demand growth and rising non-OPEC+ supply, which would likely keep WTI trading in the high $60s.
