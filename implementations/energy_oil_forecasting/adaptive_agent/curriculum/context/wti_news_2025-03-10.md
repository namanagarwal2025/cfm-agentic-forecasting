### Price Level and Recent Trend
West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices have experienced a sharp downward trend in early March 2025, hitting multi-month lows. After averaging around $71.21 per barrel in February, WTI fell to $68.37 on March 3 and plummeted to close at $66.31 per barrel on March 5. This sell-off has been accelerated by a larger-than-expected build in U.S. commercial crude inventories, which rose by 3.6 million barrels to 433.8 million barrels in early March, far exceeding market expectations.

### OPEC+ Production Decisions
On the supply side, OPEC+ has decided to move forward with unwinding its production cuts. During a virtual meeting on March 3, 2025, eight key member states—including Saudi Arabia, Russia, and the UAE—reaffirmed their plan to begin a gradual and flexible return of 2.2 million barrels per day (bpd) of voluntary cuts starting on April 1, 2025. The initial phase-out will see production targets rise by 138,000 bpd in April. While the alliance emphasized that these increases are adaptable and can be paused or reversed depending on market conditions, the decision to add barrels to the market has added significant downward pressure on prices.

### Geopolitical Supply Risks and Policy Shifts
Geopolitical risks and shifting U.S. policies are heavily influencing market sentiment. The implementation of new U.S. tariffs on Canada, Mexico, and China has triggered immediate retaliatory measures, raising fears of a global trade war that could stifle economic activity. Meanwhile, the U.S. administration's revocation of Chevron's operating license in Venezuela threatens to remove up to 200,000 bpd of supply from the market. These supply-side disruptions are balanced against ongoing Middle East tensions—including Houthi attacks in the Red Sea and escalating U.S.-Iran friction—and speculation that a potential resolution to the Russia-Ukraine conflict could eventually ease sanctions and return Russian crude to global markets.
