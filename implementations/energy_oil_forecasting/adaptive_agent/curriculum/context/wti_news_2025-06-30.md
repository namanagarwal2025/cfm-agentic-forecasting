In late June 2025, West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices experienced a sharp correction, dipping below $65.00 per barrel to settle at $65.17 on June 25. This downward trend capped a highly volatile month. In early June, WTI had broken above key technical resistance to settle around $64.60, before geopolitical flare-ups in the middle of the month drove a rapid rally, pushing benchmark prices up by several dollars (with WTI rising toward $76 per barrel). However, by the final week of June, a sudden de-escalation triggered a massive sell-off, with WTI dropping roughly 17% from its mid-month peak as the geopolitical risk premium quickly unwound.

On the supply side, OPEC+ has committed to a gradual and flexible unwinding of its voluntary production cuts. Following their virtual meeting on May 31, 2025, eight core member countries—including Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, and Kuwait—reaffirmed their plans to return 2.2 million barrels per day (bpd) of voluntary adjustments to the market. The group agreed to implement a production increase of 411,000 bpd for July 2025 compared to June levels. OPEC+ emphasized that these monthly increments remain highly flexible and can be paused or reversed depending on prevailing market conditions to maintain overall stability.

Geopolitical risks heavily dominated market sentiment throughout June 2025. Tensions in the Middle East spiked dramatically following Israel's June 13 air strikes on Iranian military and nuclear facilities, which initially stoked severe fears of supply disruptions and potential threats to the Strait of Hormuz—a vital global transit choke point. These anxieties were significantly alleviated on June 24, 2025, when Israel and Iran agreed to a United States-brokered ceasefire. The announcement of the truce immediately eased supply fears, prompting traders to rapidly liquidate long positions and returning the market's focus to physical supply-and-demand fundamentals.

The global demand outlook for the remainder of 2025 remains mixed.
