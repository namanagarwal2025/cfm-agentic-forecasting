**Price Level and Recent Trend**
West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices have experienced a downward trend, trading in the high $50s per barrel (around $59/bbl) by late November and early December 2025. This represents a multi-year low, with prices dipping to levels not seen since early 2021. The bearish sentiment is primarily driven by a persistent global supply surplus, rising inventories, and a slowing global economy, particularly in China. Although prices have seen brief, volatile rebounds in response to geopolitical developments, the overall market structure has flirted with contango, reflecting a well-supplied physical market.
